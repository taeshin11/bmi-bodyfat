2. Description of Service

BMI Dashboard is a free, web-based health and fitness tool that provides users with the ability to calculate their Body Mass Index (BMI) and estimate their body fat percentage. The BMI calculation is based on the standard formula endorsed by the World Health Organization (WHO), which divides an individual's weight in kilograms by the square of their height in meters. The body fat percentage estimation uses the US Navy circumference method, which derives an estimate based on gender-specific body circumference measurements including the neck, waist, and (for females) hip measurements.

The Service supports both metric (kilograms and centimeters) and imperial (pounds, feet, and inches) unit systems and provides results along with general health category classifications based on WHO and other recognized standards. BMI Dashboard operates entirely in the user's web browser, meaning all calculations are performed client-side using JavaScript. No health data is transmitted to or stored on our servers. The Service also includes educational content about BMI categories, body fat percentage ranges, and general health and fitness information. All features of the Service are provided free of charge, and no user registration or account creation is required.

4. Medical Disclaimer

BMI Dashboard does not provide medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment. This is the most important section of these Terms, and we urge you to read it carefully. The calculations, results, health category classifications, educational text, and all other content on this website are provided solely for general informational and educational purposes. They are not intended to serve as a substitute for professional medical advice, clinical diagnosis, or medical treatment of any kind.

Body Mass Index (BMI) and body fat percentage are population-level screening tools and statistical estimates, not clinical diagnostic instruments. These metrics have well-documented limitations that are important to understand. BMI does not distinguish between muscle mass and fat mass, which means that muscular individuals may be classified as "overweight" or "obese" despite having a healthy body composition. BMI may also be less accurate for certain populations, including but not limited to: professional and amateur athletes, elderly individuals experiencing age-related muscle loss (sarcopenia), pregnant or breastfeeding women, growing children and adolescents, and individuals of certain ethnic backgrounds for whom standard BMI thresholds may not be appropriate.

The US Navy circumference method for estimating body fat percentage, while validated in research studies, relies on external body circumference measurements that are inherently subject to measurement error. Factors such as tape placement, posture, breathing, hydration level, recent meals, and time of day can all influence the accuracy of circumference measurements and, consequently, the body fat percentage estimate. Furthermore, neither BMI nor the US Navy method accounts for fat distribution within the body (for example, the difference between visceral fat surrounding internal organs and subcutaneous fat beneath the skin), which is an important factor in assessing metabolic health risk.

Always consult a qualified healthcare professional — such as a licensed physician, registered dietitian, certified clinical nutritionist, or certified fitness professional — before making any decisions or changes regarding your diet, exercise regimen, weight management plan, or overall health management strategy. Never disregard professional medical advice or delay seeking medical consultation because of something you have read on or calculated through this website. If you are experiencing a medical emergency, contact your local emergency services (such as 911 in the United States) immediately. BMI Dashboard is not designed for and should not be used in emergency medical situations.
